Prerequisites · self-assessment

What you need to start.

You do not need a PhD. You need three things: vectors, dot products, and the idea that a function can produce a probability distribution. Check them off below — there's a quick quiz for each.

The checklist

  • Vectors as lists of numbers
    A vector is an ordered list. [0.9, 0.1, 0.2, -0.1] is a 4-vector. No geometry required.
  • The dot product
    Multiply element-wise and sum: $\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = \sum_i a_i b_i$. Result is one number.
  • Matrix multiplication
    $AB_{ij} = \sum_k A_{ik} B_{kj}$. Each output cell is a dot product of one row of $A$ with one column of $B$.
  • Softmax turns scores into probabilities
    $\text{softmax}(x_i) = e^{x_i} / \sum_j e^{x_j}$. All outputs are positive and sum to 1.
