Part III · §5

Positional encodings, a choir of sinusoids.

Attention is permutation-invariant. Shuffle the tokens and the math is unchanged — a disaster for language, where order is meaning. The Transformer's fix: add a position-dependent vector to each token embedding before the first attention layer.

The formula

Sinusoidal positional encoding
$$ \mathrm{PE}_{(\mathit{pos}, 2i)} = \sin\!\left(\frac{\mathit{pos}}{10000^{2i/d}}\right) $$ $$ \mathrm{PE}_{(\mathit{pos}, 2i+1)} = \cos\!\left(\frac{\mathit{pos}}{10000^{2i/d}}\right) $$

Each dimension is a sinusoid at a different frequency — fast wiggles in low dimensions, slow ones in high dimensions. The wavelengths form a geometric progression from $2\pi$ to $10000 \cdot 2\pi$.

Two reasons this works. Uniqueness: no two positions get the same vector, so attention can tell them apart. Linear relative offset: $\mathrm{PE}_{pos+k}$ is a linear function of $\mathrm{PE}_{pos}$ — the model can learn relative-distance attention with a single linear projection.

Experiment 1 — The PE matrix, visualized

One row per position, one column per dimension.

Cool blues are $-1$, warm reds are $+1$. Low-dim columns flicker quickly, high-dim columns barely change. That's the geometric frequency progression made visible.

Figure 1 · PE for 32 positions × 64 dimensions

x-axis: dimension (0…63). y-axis: position (0…31). Each row is one token's added positional vector.

Fig 1 — at low dimensions the rows differ a lot row-to-row; at high dimensions, hardly at all. Every position still gets a unique fingerprint across all 64.

Experiment 3 — Position similarity

How similar are two positions?

Compute $\mathrm{PE}_{i} \cdot \mathrm{PE}_{j}$ for every pair $(i,j)$. Result: a banded matrix that depends only on $|i-j|$ — i.e., on relative distance. This is the property that lets attention learn relative-position rules.

Figure 3 · $\mathrm{PE}\,\mathrm{PE}^\top$ for 32 positions

Bright diagonal: same position. Bands parallel to it: pairs at fixed relative distance.

Fig 3 — the diagonal-banded structure is what gives sinusoidal PE its name. Rotary PE (RoPE) achieves the same property more cleanly and now dominates large LMs.
To remember — Attention has no notion of order until you give it one. Sinusoidal PE injects position with two properties: every position is unique, and pairwise similarity depends only on relative distance.
